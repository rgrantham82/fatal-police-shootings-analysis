# Portfolio Talking Points

## One-sentence version

I cleaned and modeled fatal police shooting data, merged population denominators for per-capita analysis, built predictive and statistical models, identified New Mexico as a major outlier, and developed a focused Albuquerque dashboard to investigate local concentration and agency involvement.

## Skills demonstrated

- Python data cleaning
- Public dataset integration
- Population-normalized rate construction
- Statistical testing
- Negative binomial count modeling
- Predictive model evaluation
- Dashboard design in Excel
- Geographic and policy-focused exploratory analysis
- Written analytical reporting

## Interview-ready summary

The project started as a national fatal police shooting analysis but became more interesting once raw counts were adjusted for population. California has the largest raw count, but New Mexico is much more striking on a per-capita basis. I used observed-vs-expected comparisons, regional tests, and count models to show that New Mexico remains high even after accounting for region and year. Then I focused on Albuquerque because it accounts for about a third of New Mexico's records. The next step is to add true exposure denominators such as calls for service, arrests, violent crime, officer staffing, and mental-health crisis calls.

## Strong caveat to say out loud

This is not a causal proof of misconduct or danger. It is an exploratory analysis showing where rates are unusually high and where deeper data collection is justified.
